Michael J. Martinez, a technology reporter for ABC News, discusses the relationship between hackers and the media. He highlights the challenges of covering an underground community like hacking and how it leads to misconceptions and biases in reporting. Martinez offers insights into the media's perspective, including the pressure to generate readership and the impact of deadlines. He suggests that hackers can improve their media portrayal by providing more information, explaining their motivations, and distinguishing themselves from malicious actors. The talk concludes with a Q&A session, addressing topics such as the media's use of the term "hacker" versus "cracker" and the responsibility of reporting on potential exploits.
