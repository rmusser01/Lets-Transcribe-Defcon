The speaker, Greg (aka XS), introduces the Lightweight Directory Access Protocol (LDAP), an open-standard alternative to the X.500 Directory Access Protocol. LDAP is designed to address the challenges of centralized databases, offering decentralized maintenance and powerful search capabilities. Greg discusses the definition, security, and common uses of LDAP, highlighting its flexibility in storing data and compatibility with various platforms and applications. He also mentions open-source LDAP servers, such as OpenLDAP, and addresses some implementation challenges and potential bugs. The presentation aims to provide a comprehensive overview of LDAP and its potential applications.
