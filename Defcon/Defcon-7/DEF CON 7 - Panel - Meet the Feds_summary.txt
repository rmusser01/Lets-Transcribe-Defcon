At the 1999 DEFCON convention in Las Vegas, a panel featuring federal agents and cybersecurity experts discusses the evolving landscape of cyber threats and the challenges of ensuring national security in the digital age. The panel acknowledges the government's struggle to keep up with the rapidly advancing technology, the increasing number of cyber attacks, and the difficulty of attributing these attacks to specific individuals or groups. They emphasize the potential consequences of cyber attacks, likening them to a new form of weaponry in an arms race. The discussion also touches on legal and ethical considerations, including the need to protect privacy rights while also addressing the growing threat of cyber crimes. The panel highlights the importance of collaboration between the government and private sector in addressing these challenges and encourages attendees with hacking expertise to consider using their skills for the betterment of cybersecurity.
