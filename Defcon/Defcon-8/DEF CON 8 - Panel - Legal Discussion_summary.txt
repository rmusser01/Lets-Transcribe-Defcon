A federal prosecutor and a civil attorney discuss the legal implications of computer crimes, including stealing, malicious behavior, and reverse engineering. They emphasize the importance of intent and damages in determining criminal and civil liability. The prosecutor distinguishes between federal and local jurisdiction and clarifies the $5,000 threshold for federal prosecution. The civil attorney predicts an increase in civil litigation against hackers and internet security companies. They advise on risk mitigation and insurance for security professionals. The discussion also covers counter-attacks, negligence, and international hacking incidents. Audience questions address due diligence, evidence preservation, and the impact of system banners on privacy expectations.
