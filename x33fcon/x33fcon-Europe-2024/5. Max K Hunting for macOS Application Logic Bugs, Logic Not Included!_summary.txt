In this talk, the speaker, a security consultant, shares their experience hunting for logic bugs in macOS applications. They detail their process, including reverse engineering, analyzing launch daemons and plists, and understanding XPC and NSXPC services. They also highlight the importance of responsible disclosure and provide insights into their interactions with vendors during the bug reporting process. The presentation covers three exploitable bugs found in Zscaler, AWS VPN, and F-Secure products, as well as two unexploitable logic bugs. The speaker emphasizes the prevalence of logic bugs and the importance of breaking assumptions to identify vulnerabilities. They also offer recommendations for improving the bug hunting process and engaging with vendors.
