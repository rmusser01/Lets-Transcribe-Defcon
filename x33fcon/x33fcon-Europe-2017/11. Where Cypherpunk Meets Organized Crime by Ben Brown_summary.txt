The talk provides a historical overview of cybercrime, from telegram fraud in the late 1800s to modern darknet markets. The focus is on financially motivated electronic crimes, including scams, purchasing fraud, and drug sales. The presenter discusses the evolution of networks, from ARPANET to the modern internet, and the emergence of hacker collectives in the 1980s and 1990s. Law enforcement responses to cybercrime are also covered, including global operations and changes in legislation. The presentation concludes by raising concerns about the future of privacy and security for both criminals and regular citizens in the context of ongoing Crypto Wars.
