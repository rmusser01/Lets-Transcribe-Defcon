In his talk, Chris Nickerson discusses the concept of security performance engineering and how it can improve an organization's security posture. He reflects on his diverse career in security, highlighting the ineffectiveness of traditional security assessments in creating sustainable change. Chris emphasizes the need for metrics and measurements to evaluate security controls, detect decay, and improve quality and efficiency. He suggests using the MITRE ATT&CK framework as a common language for both offense and defense, and proposes a process to map controls, test effectiveness, and measure quality and efficiency. By providing data-driven insights, security teams can empower organizations to make better decisions and improve their security posture over time.
