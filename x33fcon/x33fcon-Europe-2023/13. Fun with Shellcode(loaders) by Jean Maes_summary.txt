In this talk, Jean Maes shares insights and lessons learned from his journey in pen testing and red teaming. He introduces the audience to Jimmy, a novice pen tester who encounters challenges while trying to hack his ex-girlfriend's system. Jimmy's story serves as a backdrop to discuss various concepts and techniques in pen testing. Jean emphasizes the importance of understanding static and dynamic detections, antivirus software, and endpoint detection and response (EDR) tools. He also discusses the benefits and drawbacks of staged and stageless payloads, the role of shellcode in evading detections, and the impact of language choice on malware development. Additionally, Jean offers design considerations for creating loaders, including staging strategies, language selection, and on-disk versus in-memory techniques. The presentation concludes with OPSEC tips and highlights the importance of knowing one's tools to ensure effective and stealthy red teaming operations.
